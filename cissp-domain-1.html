Security and Risk Management

The foundation of the entire CISSP — aligning security to business strategy through governance, professional ethics, law and compliance, security policy, risk management, business continuity, personnel security, and the awareness programmes that build the human firewall.

Sub-domains

Exam outline areas.

Domain 1 is the heaviest-weighted domain (16%) and sets the conceptual foundation for all the others — twelve outline areas covering ethics, core security concepts, governance, law, policy, risk management, business continuity, personnel security, and security awareness.

1.1 — Professional ethics

Understand, adhere to and promote professional ethics — the (ISC)² Code of Professional Ethics and organisational codes of conduct.

1.2 — Apply security concepts

The CIA triad (confidentiality, integrity, availability), plus authenticity, non-repudiation, and the (DAD / IAAA) foundations.

1.3 — Security governance principles

Aligning security to business strategy, roles & responsibilities, due care / due diligence, and governance frameworks.

1.4 — Legal, regulatory & compliance

Cybercrime, licensing & IP, import/export controls, transborder data flow, and privacy law in a holistic context.

1.5 — Investigation types

Administrative, criminal, civil, regulatory and industry-standard investigations and their differing requirements.

1.6 — Security policy & documents

Develop and implement policy, standards, procedures, baselines and guidelines — and which are mandatory.

1.7 — Business Continuity requirements

Identify, analyse, assess, prioritise and implement BC requirements — including the BIA.

1.8 — Personnel security

Hiring (screening), employment (NDAs, AUP), and termination — plus separation of duties and job rotation.

1.9 — Risk management concepts

Identify and assess risk, ALE / SLE / ARO, risk treatment (mitigate / accept / transfer / avoid), and frameworks.

1.10 — Threat modeling

Apply threat-modeling concepts and methodologies such as STRIDE, PASTA and DREAD.

1.11 — Supply Chain Risk Management

Apply SCRM concepts — vetting suppliers, fourth-party risk, and security in contracts and SLAs.

1.12 — Awareness, training & education

Establish and maintain a programme across the three levels — awareness, training and education.
