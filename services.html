V-CISO Services

Virtual CISO (V-CISO) advisory provides organizations with strategic security leadership on a flexible, part-time or project basis. This service is ideal for companies that need board-level security governance, risk oversight, and compliance direction without the overhead of a full-time CISO hire.

Engagements typically include security program design, policy development, risk register management, vendor risk oversight, incident response planning, and executive reporting. The V-CISO acts as a trusted advisor to leadership, translating technical risk into business-relevant insight and decision-ready recommendations.

  • Security program roadmap and maturity assessment
  • Board-level reporting and executive dashboards
  • Risk register creation and periodic review
  • Policy and procedure framework development
  • Vendor and third-party risk management
  • Incident response planning and tabletop exercises
  • Security awareness and training strategy
  • Regulatory and compliance alignment advisory
Strategic Advisory Board Reporting Risk Governance Flexible Engagement

Cybersecurity Tools Planning & Services

This service provides end-to-end advisory on selecting, deploying, and operationalizing cybersecurity tools. From SIEM/XDR to vulnerability scanners, endpoint protection, and cloud security platforms - the engagement ensures your security stack aligns with your risk profile, budget, and operational maturity.

Engagements cover tool evaluation, architecture design, integration planning, deployment support, and operational playbook development. The goal is to move beyond tool procurement into measurable security outcomes - detection coverage, response capability, and compliance-aligned visibility.

  • Security stack assessment and gap analysis
  • Tool evaluation and vendor comparison
  • SIEM/XDR architecture and deployment planning
  • Vulnerability management program design
  • Endpoint protection strategy and rollout
  • Cloud security posture management (CSPM)
  • Operational playbook and runbook development
  • Integration and automation advisory
SIEM / XDR Vulnerability Management Cloud Security Tooling Strategy

DPDPA Implementation

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023 establishes India's framework for personal data protection. This service helps organizations achieve compliance through structured data governance, consent management, and privacy-by-design implementation aligned with DPDPA requirements.

Engagements cover data discovery and mapping, lawful processing assessments, consent mechanism design, Data Protection Officer (DPO) advisory, data breach notification processes, and cross-border transfer compliance. The outcome is a defensible, audit-ready privacy program that meets regulatory expectations.

  • Personal data inventory and data flow mapping
  • Lawful purpose and consent mechanism design
  • Privacy impact assessments (PIA)
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO) advisory
  • Data breach notification procedures
  • Cross-border data transfer compliance
  • Privacy policy and notice development
  • Organizational readiness and training
DPDPA 2023 Data Privacy Consent Management Compliance Readiness

Gap Assessment for Cybersecurity Regulatory Audits

This service provides structured gap assessments against cybersecurity requirements mandated by Indian financial and sectoral regulators - including RBI, IRDA, SEBI, and other regulatory bodies. The assessment identifies control gaps, prioritizes remediation, and builds a clear path to audit readiness.

Engagements cover regulatory requirement mapping, current-state control evaluation, risk-rated gap reporting, remediation roadmaps, and pre-audit preparation support. The approach ensures organizations are not just compliant on paper, but operationally prepared for regulatory scrutiny.

  • Regulatory requirement mapping (RBI, IRDA, SEBI)
  • Current-state control assessment
  • Gap analysis and risk-rated findings
  • Remediation roadmap and prioritization
  • Policy and procedure alignment
  • Evidence collection and documentation
  • Pre-audit readiness review
  • Management reporting and executive summary
RBI IRDA SEBI Audit Readiness

SOC 2 Readiness & Attestation

System and Organization Controls (SOC) 2 is a recognized auditing standard for service providers securely managing data to protect organizational interests and client privacy. This service supports organizations preparing for SOC 2 Type 1 and Type 2 attestations.

The engagement covers defining Trust Services Criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy), mapping existing controls, identifying design gaps, implementing remediation strategies, and supporting the final CPA assessment to ensure a successful audit outcome.

  • Trust Services Criteria (TSC) scoping
  • Current-state control mapping and gap analysis
  • Control design and implementation guidance
  • Policy and procedure documentation
  • Evidence collection and readiness review
  • Vendor management and third-party risk assessment
  • Pre-audit mock assessment
  • CPA audit facilitation and support
SOC 2 Type 1 & 2 Trust Services Criteria Audit Preparation

ISO 27001 Implementation & Certification

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). This service covers end-to-end implementation - from initial scoping and gap analysis through risk assessment, control selection, policy development, and Stage 1/Stage 2 certification audit readiness.

The engagement aligns your organization's security controls with Annex A requirements, builds a risk treatment plan, and establishes ongoing measurement and continual improvement processes. Whether you're pursuing first-time certification or transitioning to the 2022 revision, the approach is structured, practical, and business-aligned.

  • ISMS scoping and context of the organization
  • Gap analysis against ISO 27001:2022 controls
  • Risk assessment methodology and risk treatment plan
  • Statement of Applicability (SoA) development
  • Policy, procedure, and documentation framework
  • Internal audit planning and execution
  • Management review and continual improvement
  • Stage 1 and Stage 2 audit preparation support
ISMS Annex A Controls Risk Treatment Certification Readiness

ISO 22301 Implementation & Certification

ISO 22301 is the international standard for Business Continuity Management Systems (BCMS). This service helps organizations establish, maintain, and certify a structured approach to business continuity - ensuring critical operations can withstand and recover from disruptive events.

The engagement covers business impact analysis (BIA), risk assessment, recovery strategy development, business continuity plan (BCP) documentation, exercise and testing programs, and ongoing monitoring. The outcome is a resilient, auditable continuity framework that meets certification requirements and protects stakeholder confidence.

  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA) and criticality mapping
  • Risk assessment for continuity threats
  • Recovery strategy and RTO/RPO alignment
  • Business continuity plan documentation
  • Crisis communication procedures
  • Exercise planning, testing, and lessons learned
  • Internal audit and management review
  • Certification audit preparation
BCMS BIA Disaster Recovery Organizational Resilience

It is designed for organizations deploying or developing AI systems that require structured governance, regulatory alignment, and stakeholder assurance.

  • Data governance and quality controls
  • Bias, fairness, and transparency frameworks
  • AI system lifecycle management
  • Accountability and oversight structures
  • Documentation and policy development
  • Certification readiness and audit support
AI Governance Responsible AI Risk Controls AIMS

ISO 17021 Implementation & Certification

ISO/IEC 17021 specifies requirements for bodies providing audit and certification of management systems. This service supports certification bodies in achieving and maintaining accreditation by ensuring their operations meet the standard's requirements for competence, consistency, and impartiality.

The engagement covers auditor competence frameworks, impartiality risk management, certification process design, nonconformity management, and quality management system alignment. It is critical for organizations that certify others and must demonstrate credibility to accreditation bodies and the market.

  • Certification body process design and documentation
  • Auditor competence evaluation framework
  • Impartiality risk assessment and controls
  • Nonconformity and corrective action processes
  • Certification decision-making procedures
  • Surveillance and recertification planning
  • Internal audit and management review
  • Accreditation body assessment preparation
Accreditation Certification Bodies Impartiality Conformity Assessment

The engagement addresses organizational structure, independence and impartiality requirements, inspection methods and procedures, quality system development, records management, and complaint-handling processes. It ensures inspection bodies can demonstrate technical competence and operational reliability.

  • Inspection body organizational structure review
  • Independence and impartiality assessment
  • Inspection method and procedure development
  • Personnel competence and qualification framework
  • Quality system documentation
  • Equipment and resource management processes
  • Complaint and appeal handling procedures
  • Accreditation readiness assessment
Inspection Bodies Accreditation Quality Systems Competence

ISO/IEC 17025 specifies requirements for the competence of testing and calibration laboratories. This service helps laboratories establish management systems and technical competence, ensuring their results are valid, reliable, and internationally recognized.

The engagement includes method validation, measurement uncertainty estimation, equipment calibration management, personnel competence assessment, quality control procedures, and accreditation body assessment preparation. It covers both the management system and technical requirements of the standard.

  • Laboratory quality management system design
  • Method validation and verification
  • Measurement uncertainty estimation
  • Equipment calibration and maintenance programs
  • Personnel competence and training framework
  • Proficiency testing participation planning
  • Quality control and assurance procedures
  • Accreditation assessment preparation

  • Risk-based thinking and opportunity analysis
  • Documented information framework
